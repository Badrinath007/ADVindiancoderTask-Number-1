Enhancement of UWB Patch Antenna using Metamaterial for MIMO System

Ultra-wideband (UWB) technology has gained significant attention in recent years due to its numerous advantages over traditional narrowband systems, including high data rates, low power consumption, and robustness to interference. Microstrip antennas are widely used in UWB applications due to their compact size, ease of integration, and broad bandwidth.

Metamaterial-based antenna designs have shown potential in improving the performance of multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) systems. These designs use structures that can manipulate electromagnetic waves, resulting in improved bandwidth, gain, and isolation between antenna elements. Designs have demonstrated improved performance compared to traditional antennas, making them suitable for use in MIMO systems operating in various frequency bands.

Microstrip antennas are widely used in modern wireless communication systems due to their low profile, lightweight, and ease of integration with microwave circuits. A microstrip antenna consists of a thin metallic patch printed on a dielectric substrate and fed by a coaxial cable or a microstrip line. Microstrip antennas have several advantages over traditional antennas, such as omnidirectional radiation pattern, low cost, and conformal nature. However, microstrip antennas also suffer from several challenges, such as narrow bandwidth, low gain, and low radiation efficiency.

To overcome these challenges, researchers have explored the use of ultra- wideband (UWB) technology in microstrip antenna designs. UWB technology allows for the transmission of signals with very large bandwidths, typically greater than 500 MHz or 20% of the center frequency. UWB signals have several advantages over narrowband signals, such as increased data rate, improved signal-to-noise ratio, and reduced interference. UWB technology has applications in various fields, such as wireless sensor networks, radar systems, and high-speed data transmission. The combination of microstrip antennas and UWB technology has led to the development of UWB microstrip antennas, which have several advantages over traditional microstrip antennas. UWB microstrip antennas have larger bandwidth, higher gain, and improved radiation efficiency, making them suitable for use in various applications, such as communication systems, medical imaging, and environmental sensing.

H-slotting is a technique used to modify the performance of microstrip patch antennas by introducing a narrow slot in the shape of an "H" in the radiating patch element. This slot modifies the electromagnetic field distribution on the patch, leading to changes in the antenna's radiation characteristics.

One advantage of using H-slotting in microstrip patch antennas is that it can improve the impedance bandwidth and reduce the cross-polarization levels. This makes the antenna more suitable for use in wireless communication systems that require high data rates and reliable performance.

In conclusion, our new proposed design metamaterial-based antenna design for MIMO system applications, which used a complementary split-ring resonator (CSRR) structure to improve the antenna performance. The proposed antenna design was based on a monopole antenna placed in close proximity to another monopole antenna on a dielectric substrate. The CSRR structure was integrated into the ground plane of the antenna to reduce mutual coupling and enhance the antenna's performance.

The bandwidth of the proposed antenna is about 7.2 GHz and radiation efficiency is 79%. The radiated power at 7.8 GHz frequency is 0.948 watts and peak directivity is 1.021.

Channel Optimization for 5g NR

In the growing wireless communication systems of 5G, FBMC is one the of the dominant contenders and the final successor of 5G which is 5G NR (New Radio) a flexible and standalone version of 5G. After the implementation of Multiple Access techniques, most of the situations the performance of the system doesn’t rely completely on the transmitter and receiver but also depends on the channel whose performance can be improved by increasing SNR (Signal to Noise Ratio) & decreasing the BER (Bit Error Rate).

Channel,Transmitter & Receiver altogether give rise to a better performing communication system. In this project we worked on improving channel and propose a channel with lesser bit error and high data rates which are the basic requirements of a robust channel. We have worked with the following parameters, Subcarrier Spacing, Carrier Frequency, No. of Subcarriers, Overlapping Factors and Hermite coefficients.

In today’s era of wireless communication, users demand high speed and low latency network. To fulfill the demands of the subscribers, FBMC-OQAM modulation technique has been used and this give rise to 5g wireless communication. FBMC-OQAM has been taken into consideration to implement and to get good data rates as compared to 4G. In 4G, OFDM technique has been used as the modulation technique to increase the data rates and reduce the latency with efficient bandwidth usage.

In OFDM, CP (Cyclic prefix) has been used to prevent loss of data that is transmitted. The poor spectral properties of OFDM are one of its biggest disadvantages so that we have switched to FBMC with OQAM, in short just FBMC, has lower out-of-band emissions than OFDM. 5G NR (New Radio) is a new technology which has been developed by 3GPP (3 rd Group Partnership Project) from 5G to improve its efficiency when compared with 4G. 5G NR is a successor of 5G with the following features, Stand Alone – Unlike 5G, which has a backward compatibility to almost all 4G devices, 5G NR which only supports devices that are created specifically for 5G. It’s expected to have lower cost, better efficiency, and to assist development of new use cases as the old protocols for 4G will be dropped for good. Improved Spectral Efficiency – With 4G out of the equation, 5G spectrum can completely utilize its spectrum only for 5G rather than multiplexing it with 4G LTE spectrum. Increased number of Subcarrier Spacing – 5G NR supports 5 subcarrier frequencies [15KHz, 30KHz, 60KHz, 120KHz & 240KHz], whereas 5G uses the same subcarrier spacing (15KHz) as 4G LTE for backward compatibility.

FBMC is implemented in 5G, which is as type of modulation that uses Filter Bank with Multi Carriers to modulate data and it has been developed from ODFM. In FBMC, Cyclic Prefix are replaced by Filter Bank. Filter Bank is an array of bandpass filters that splits the input signal into multiple components, each one carrying a single frequency sub-band of the message signal. These sub-bands are obtained by applying FFT to the oversampled message signal. These filter banks are distributed in frequency domain, which is more likely the same as in OFDM.

FBMC is a modified technique of OFDM to control the impacts of ISI which is one of the great challenges in the wireless communication system. The efficiency of the system relies on the ideas implied for BER reduction. From our experimentation all this time we say that, for 30KHz subcarrier spacing with new Hermite coefficients, overlapping factor, number of subcarriers and by increasing carrier frequency from 2.4GHz to 4.2GHz gives better error reduction which gives rise to higher data rates when implemented and implemented which is simulated and inferred using MATLAB. Thus, with the subcarrier spacing as 30KHz, FBMC channel provides a stronger SNR and lower BER compared to that of the existing work. Also importing some features of 5G NR, increased compatibility and higher data rates can be achieved.

Personal Expenses Tracker Application

Personal expense tracker application will maintain data of daily, weekly, monthly or yearly expenses. The expenses and earnings are maintained in a simple and intuitive way thus making the user to access the required categories easily making it an eco-friendly application.

Personal Expense Tracker is an essential application in our day-to-day life which helps users keep track of their expenses in every mode of transaction they use i.e. (E-pays, Debit Cards, UPI …etc.). Instead of Blindly spending the money it helps the user to keep track of how much money spent and can create a deadline to manage their expenses. Using this application user can frame a budgeting plan and can keep track of their expenses in an organized way. This helps the user to maintain a proper balance between their budgeting plan and expenditure.

Un - Dee is an expense tracker application which helps the users to maintain a perfect balance between their expenses and budgeting plan. This application helps the user to create a budget plan by considering their necessary expenses and by viewing their previous month’s budget plan and build an efficient budget design based on it. When the user’s expenses reach the budgeting plan’s threshold limit the user will be notified and the user can change their expenses or budgeting plan based on it. Digital transactions like card payments, upi’s are taken into account directly and the non-digital payments should be entered by the user manually.

Site is functional using FLASK module to construct. Login page and sign in send and fetch data from ibm_db2. Every page is protected against unintended entering of a webpage. Dashboard shows the basic info about the user and their expenditure using various kinds of graphs and APIs and all other webpages are honed for quality improvisation and for best visual experience.

The functional block of this project is both Docker and Kubernetes. Docker is to containerize the flask application and Kubernetes is to deploy the site using the docker image and to expose the site publicly. There are a lot of constraints when implementing Docker and Kubernetes due of the complexity of having 2 different containers in the same pod. Because of the DHCP server and other performance issues.
Note: The project had been deployed on IBM cloud server and it is no more available due to trial expired.
