Enhancement of UWB Patch Antenna using Metamaterial for MIMO System

Ultra-wideband (UWB) technology has gained significant attention in recent years due to its numerous advantages over traditional narrowband systems, including high data rates, low power consumption, and robustness to interference. Microstrip antennas are widely used in UWB applications due to their compact size, ease of integration, and broad bandwidth.

Metamaterial-based antenna designs have shown potential in improving the performance of multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) systems. These designs use structures that can manipulate electromagnetic waves, resulting in improved bandwidth, gain, and isolation between antenna elements.
